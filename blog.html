Reflection on the reading:

“Convention and Context, Mailoux, 1983” was definitely an interesting and insightful read, it reminds the designers of the importance of considering both established design conventions and the specific context of users when creating digital interfaces. While we as designers should be familiar with the principles and patterns that users have come to expect with websites, they must also be attuned to the specific needs, goals, and audiences of their design projects. By balancing the two, we can create experiences for the user that are intuitive, user-friendly, and tailored to the content presented. Mailoux's emphasis on the interplay between convention and context encourages us to continually reassess and iterate upon our work, ensuring that their designs evolve in response to ever-changing user expectations and technological advancements.

- 2466437 - Lucien Zhang - Week 7 - Blog Post

Experience with continued development of my website:

This week I have pretty much finalised the layout of my website and have made considerable progress on both the HTML and CSS, most of my pages are pretty much done or are getting there. There were not many changes I made this week, however I do need to update my wireframe to fit with the new layout as it is quite different from the original designs, but other than that my website will be finalised fairly soon

Reflection on the IxD Process Lecture:

Goal Alignment: My goal with the website is to create a modern, minimalist and elegant style that is easy to navigate for the viewers and is easy to read. The users I designed this website for are one for the markers, two for like minded individuals that I may potentially form partnerships for future opportunities as well as for my future employment as a web designer. My content is based on this course’s work as well as to show my decision making and growth as a web designer for my future portfolio.

Information structure: The information structure of my website is clear and simple with minimal amount of distractions for the viewer, all the information is structured to be easily read and understood and clearly marked so if one wishes to seek out a specific section of my work, it should be relatively easy.

User Flow: The user flow of my website is designed to be nonlinear, the user is first presented with the home page that displays a small, but relative information about me and then they are free to explorer the different pages as they see if, the information of each page are displayed linearly, however everything is clearly labelled so that if they wish to seek out a specific piece of information, they could easily find it.

Interface Elements: Due to my website being relatively simple, the only interface elements are the buttons to swap from page to page, I find too many interface elements a page to be cluttered and often causes confusion for the viewer.

Design: My design document is getting filled as I continue with my website. Once the document is finalised I will add it to my design page, however so far I am very much liking the design I have managed to create. The wireframe design also needs to be updated with the changes I’ve made.

Reflection on the reading:

The main thing I got from this week’s reading is that we as designers cannot create a design that would cater to all and we can’t expect our audience to obtain the same experiences with our designs as we did. The authors argue that UX should not solely focus on usability and functionality but also on intangible, experiential aspects that can create positive, memorable, and meaningful interactions between users and technology, thus we as designers have to take a more comprehensive approach to UX designing that encompasses all aspects of user experience, ultimately leading to the creation of more engaging, enjoyable, and meaningful websites.
